Pharmacology Reference - Common Drug Classes

Statins (HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors):
Drugs: atorvastatin, rosuvastatin, simvastatin, pravastatin.
Mechanism: Inhibit HMG-CoA reductase, reducing cholesterol synthesis in liver.
Indications: Hypercholesterolemia, cardiovascular disease prevention.
Side effects: Myopathy (muscle pain/weakness), rhabdomyolysis (rare), elevated liver enzymes.
Monitoring: LFTs at baseline, CK if myopathy suspected.

ACE Inhibitors:
Drugs: lisinopril, enalapril, ramipril, perindopril.
Mechanism: Block conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II.
Indications: Hypertension, heart failure, post-MI, diabetic nephropathy.
Side effects: Dry cough (10-15%), angioedema (rare), hyperkalemia, acute kidney injury.
Contraindications: Pregnancy, bilateral renal artery stenosis.

Beta-Blockers:
Drugs: metoprolol, bisoprolol, carvedilol, atenolol, propranolol.
Mechanism: Block beta-adrenergic receptors.
Indications: Hypertension, angina, heart failure (HFrEF), post-MI, arrhythmias.
Side effects: Bradycardia, fatigue, bronchospasm (non-selective), sexual dysfunction.
Contraindications: Severe bradycardia, high-degree AV block, decompensated heart failure, severe asthma.

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists:
Drugs: semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide, exenatide.
Mechanism: Mimic glucagon-like peptide-1, enhance glucose-dependent insulin secretion.
Indications: Type 2 diabetes, obesity (semaglutide/liraglutide).
Benefits: Weight loss, cardiovascular outcome benefits (LEADER, SUSTAIN-6, PIONEER 6 trials).
Side effects: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea. Rare: pancreatitis, thyroid C-cell tumors.

SGLT2 Inhibitors:
Drugs: empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, canagliflozin.
Mechanism: Inhibit sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 in kidney, reducing glucose reabsorption.
Indications: Type 2 diabetes, heart failure, CKD.
Benefits: Cardiovascular and renal protective effects.
Side effects: Genital mycotic infections, DKA (rare), volume depletion.

Antibiotics - Overview:
Beta-lactams (penicillins, cephalosporins, carbapenems): Cell wall synthesis inhibitors.
Macrolides (azithromycin, clarithromycin): Protein synthesis inhibitors, atypical coverage.
Fluoroquinolones (ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin): DNA gyrase inhibitors, broad spectrum.
Tetracyclines (doxycycline): Protein synthesis inhibitors, intracellular organisms.
Always consider local resistance patterns and culture sensitivities.
