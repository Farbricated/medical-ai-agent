Respiratory Medicine Reference

Asthma:
Chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by reversible airflow obstruction, airway hyperresponsiveness, and inflammation.
Symptoms: wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, cough (especially nocturnal).
Triggers: allergens, exercise, cold air, smoke, infections, NSAIDs.
Diagnosis: spirometry showing FEV1/FVC < 0.70, reversibility with bronchodilator (>12% and >200mL improvement).
Treatment: SABA (salbutamol) for rescue. Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) for persistent asthma. Add LABA, LAMA, or biologics for severe disease. Follow stepwise approach.

COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease):
Progressive airflow limitation due to chronic inflammation from noxious particles (mainly smoking).
Includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis.
Symptoms: chronic productive cough, dyspnea on exertion, wheeze.
Diagnosis: post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC < 0.70. GOLD staging by FEV1 % predicted.
Treatment: smoking cessation is most important. SABA/SAMA for symptoms. LABA/LAMA for persistent symptoms. ICS added for frequent exacerbations. Pulmonary rehabilitation. Oxygen for severe hypoxemia.

Pneumonia:
Community-acquired pneumonia (CAP) most commonly caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae, Haemophilus influenzae, atypical organisms.
Symptoms: fever, productive cough, pleuritic chest pain, dyspnea.
Diagnosis: chest X-ray showing consolidation, elevated WBC, elevated CRP.
Treatment: amoxicillin for mild CAP, add macrolide or use respiratory fluoroquinolone for moderate/severe. Hospitalize if CURB-65 >= 2.

Pulmonary Embolism:
Blockage of pulmonary artery by thrombus, fat, or air. Life-threatening emergency.
Symptoms: sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, hemoptysis, tachycardia, hypoxia.
Diagnosis: CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA) is gold standard. D-dimer to rule out in low probability. Wells score for pre-test probability.
Treatment: anticoagulation (LMWH, DOAC). Thrombolysis or embolectomy for massive PE with hemodynamic instability.

Shortness of Breath Differential:
Cardiac: heart failure, ACS, arrhythmia, pericardial effusion.
Pulmonary: asthma, COPD, pneumonia, PE, pneumothorax, pleural effusion.
Other: anemia, metabolic acidosis, anxiety/panic attacks.
